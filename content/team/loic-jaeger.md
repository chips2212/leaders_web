# Loïc Jaeger

## Crisis Leadership Coach & Strategic Consultant

Loïc Jaeger brings 25 years of professional experience, including 15 years leading teams in international crisis contexts and 5 years empowering leaders through coaching, mentoring, training, and strategic consulting. As an EMCC EIA Senior Practitioner, he combines deep field experience with advanced coaching credentials, specializing in building true resilience through deep trust, shared mental models, and shared purpose.

### Crisis Leadership Experience

Loïc has led missions in some of the world's most challenging environments:

- **Executive Director** for MSF Mexico and Central America (2019-2023), providing visionary leadership and creating the integrated CAMINO office, building deep trust across institutional and operational teams
- **Head of Mission** in various crises including Syria, Ukraine, South Sudan, Afghanistan, and Greece (forced displacement), creating shared mental models for teams operating in chaos
- **Field Coordinator** in conflicts in Pakistan, Colombia, and forced displacement in Liberia, establishing shared purpose that anchored teams when plans failed
- **Senior Project Leader for OD Americas**, developing community-centric operational management models that emphasized trust and psychological safety

### Coaching & Development Work

Since 2018, Loïc has provided:

- **International Coach and Mentor** (EMCC EIA Senior Practitioner level), partnering with executives to enhance leadership presence and navigate complex ethical decisions, focusing on building deep trust and shared understanding
- **Trainer specialized in leadership courses**, facilitating training for humanitarian organizations on creating shared mental models and shared purpose
- **Strategic Consultant** accompanying NGOs in multi-year strategy development, ensuring strategies are built on deep trust and shared purpose

### Key Achievements

- Successfully negotiated MSF's presence and access to vulnerable populations in challenging security environments by building deep trust with local actors
- Merged institutional and operational teams, creating the CAMINO office—a global MSF precedent—by establishing shared mental models and shared purpose
- Increased private fundraising from 6 to 20 million Mexican pesos in four years by building trust with donors and partners
- Global Executive Sponsor for DEI, initiating projects addressing structural racism by creating shared mental models around equity and inclusion
- Developed Critical Incident Management plans and responded to multiple security incidents by ensuring teams had shared mental models for coordinated response
