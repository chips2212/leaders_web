# Yaël Aberdam

## Learning Facilitator, Coach & Leadership Development Specialist

Yaël Aberdam is a Learning Facilitator, Coach, and Mentor with over 10 years of experience designing and delivering impactful training and mentoring programs tailored to humanitarian and social contexts. With a PhD in Sociology, her work emphasizes adult education, social support, and mental health, leveraging expertise in course creation, facilitation, and coaching to empower individuals and teams worldwide, with a focus on building the foundations of true resilience: deep trust, shared mental models, and shared purpose.

### Humanitarian Crisis Experience

Yaël has extensive field experience managing emergency projects and coordinating humanitarian responses:

- **Project Manager** with MSF (2005-2011) in diverse contexts, managing food and non-food distribution, nutritional projects, health care, and socio-educative programs, building deep trust with local communities and teams
- **Program Coordinator** in Ivory Coast, Burundi, and Central African Republic, leading emergency health care programs with 100-210 staff, creating shared mental models for coordinated response
- **Emergency Projects Coordinator** with SOLIDARITES in East DRC and Kosovo, managing rapid response funds and post-emergency projects, establishing shared purpose that anchored teams during chaos
- **Country Operations Coordinator** with MERLIN in Eastern DRC, overseeing 4 health care projects with 25 expatriates and 2M€ budget, building deep trust across multicultural teams
- **Emergency Desk Manager** at Comité d'Aide Médicale, responsible for portfolios in Sri Lanka, CAR, and South Sudan, creating shared mental models for effective crisis coordination

### Coaching & Facilitation Work

Since 2016, Yaël has served as:

- **Learning Facilitator-Coach and Mentor** at MSF (remote), developing comprehensive learning solutions for humanitarian professionals focused on building deep trust, shared mental models, and shared purpose
- **Capacity Building Consultant** at IOM - UN Migration in Iraq (2020), leading COVID-19 response capacity building and developing complete training packages that emphasized psychological safety and team cohesion
- **Lecturer** at Ben Gurion University (2012-2017), teaching on Migration and Refugees, incorporating lessons on building resilience through shared understanding and purpose

### Key Expertise

- Proposal and report writing (ECHO, DFID, USAID, UNICEF) that communicates shared purpose
