# Yaël Aberdam

## Learning Facilitator, Coach & Leadership Development Specialist

Yaël Aberdam is a Learning Facilitator, Coach, and Mentor with over 10 years of experience designing and delivering impactful training and mentoring programs tailored to humanitarian and social contexts. With a PhD in Sociology, her work emphasizes adult education, social support, and mental health, leveraging expertise in course creation, facilitation, and coaching to empower individuals and teams worldwide.

### Humanitarian Crisis Experience

Yaël has extensive field experience managing emergency projects and coordinating humanitarian responses:

- **Project Manager** with MSF (2005-2011) in diverse contexts, managing food and non-food distribution, nutritional projects, health care, and socio-educative programs
- **Program Coordinator** in Ivory Coast, Burundi, and Central African Republic, leading emergency health care programs with 100-210 staff
- **Emergency Projects Coordinator** with SOLIDARITES in East DRC and Kosovo, managing rapid response funds and post-emergency projects
- **Country Operations Coordinator** with MERLIN in Eastern DRC, overseeing 4 health care projects with 25 expatriates and 2M€ budget
- **Emergency Desk Manager** at Comité d'Aide Médicale, responsible for portfolios in Sri Lanka, CAR, and South Sudan

### Coaching & Facilitation Work

Since 2016, Yaël has served as:

- **Learning Facilitator-Coach and Mentor** at MSF (remote), developing comprehensive learning solutions for humanitarian professionals
- **Capacity Building Consultant** at IOM - UN Migration in Iraq (2020), leading COVID-19 response capacity building and developing complete training packages
- **Lecturer** at Ben Gurion University (2012-2017), teaching on Migration and Refugees

### Key Expertise

- Needs assessment among vulnerable populations

- Security management and risk assessment
- Financial and HR management in volatile contexts
