# Tech & AI

## When Disruption Is the Only Constant

The half-life of competitive advantage in tech has never been shorter. A new model release, a regulatory shift, or a competitor's pivot—any of these can render your strategy obsolete overnight. Your teams are building products that don't exist yet, using technologies that didn't exist two years ago, for markets that are still emerging.

We've led operations where the environment changed faster than plans could be written. Where yesterday's winning strategy became tomorrow's fatal vulnerability. The parallels between humanitarian crisis response and tech leadership are striking—and the transferrable skills are profound. Because true resilience is built on deep trust, shared mental models, and shared purpose—regardless of whether the stakes are life and death or market share.

## The Parallel: Crisis Response to Tech Disruption

| Humanitarian Crisis Context | Tech Industry Parallel |
|-----------------------------|------------------------|
| Rapidly deteriorating security situation | Sudden platform dependency or API change |
| Coordination with unreliable local partners | Managing integrations with volatile vendors |
| Operating with incomplete, contradictory intel | Making product decisions with ambiguous data |
| Supply chain disruption from border closures | Supply chain shocks (semiconductors, rare earth) |
| Team burnout from extended deployments | Burnout from sustained crunch and uncertainty |
| Ethical dilemmas with no clear rules | AI ethics, algorithmic bias, data privacy |
| Competing demands from multiple stakeholders | Balancing user needs, investors, regulators |

A rapidly scaling fintech startup discovered their core functionality depended on an API that was being deprecated with 30 days notice. Drawing on crisis protocols:

- **Immediate triage**: Assessed which features were core vs. expendable, guided by shared mental models of customer value
- **Parallel solution development**: Activated three alternative approaches simultaneously, enabled by deep trust in engineering teams
- **Stakeholder management**: Transparent communication with customers about expected disruptions, built on shared purpose that prioritizes customer relationships
- **Team stabilization**: Clear priorities prevented panic and maintained engineering velocity, enabled by shared mental models of what matters most
- **Outcome**: Seamless transition completed with zero customer churn, in 21 days, demonstrating the power of deep trust, shared mental models, and shared purpose
