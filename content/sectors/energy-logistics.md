# Energy & Logistics

## The Infrastructure of Civilization Doesn't Pause for Crisis

When pipelines rupture, ports close, or grids fail, leadership isn't about presentations—it's about execution under extreme constraints. Our coaches have led operations where infrastructure collapse was the baseline, not the exception. Convoys through contested territory. Supply chains crossing active conflict zones. Power restoration in disaster areas with no functioning government.

Your challenges are different in scale, not in kind.

## Case Study: The Port Closure

A logistics company operating in a politically volatile region faced a sudden port closure that threatened to strand $50M in inventory. Using crisis protocols developed during NGO evacuations:

- **Rapid alternative sourcing**: Activated dormant relationships and negotiated emergency overland routing
- **Customer communication**: Transparent status updates that maintained trust despite delays
- **Team stabilization**: Clear priorities that prevented panic and maintained operational focus
- **Outcome**: 80% of shipments rerouted within 72 hours, zero customer contract cancellations
